Roomful of Teeth Album Launch


Kingsley Flood Album Launch

Getting a record made these days takes hard work, perseverance and some support from your friends. Many musicians are turning to "fan-funded" schemes where supporters of the band/musicians kick in cash to help get the project made. You may have heard of Kickstarter, IndieGoGo, Pledge Music or any number of other websites that support these efforts.

We wanted to let you know about two such efforts by friends of Billsville in hopes that you would be willing to kick in some funding for these awesome musical projects. In return you'll get some great music, endless gratitude and the good feeling that comes with helping talented folks succeed. Did we mention great prizes? Because there are often great prizes.

First up is the amazing "RoomFul of Teeth" - Click here to read about this amazing group of musicians headed by founder and artisic director (and Billsville resident) Brad Wells. We got to the local performance of Roomful of Teeth every year and we're consistently blown away by everything they do. Hurry though, there are only three days left to help fund the project!

Since 2009, our ensemble of eight classically-based vocalists has been coached by masters of some of the most vivid and varied singing styles from around the world: Tuvan throat singing, Korean p'ansori singing, yodeling, and belting among others. And we have commissioned some of today's most vibrant composers – Judd Greenstein, Merrill Garbus (of tUnE-yArDs), Caleb Burhans, Sarah Kirkland Snider, Rinde Eckert, William Brittelle – to compose specifically for our mix of polyglot vocalists

Our friends Kingsley Flood are also out there with their own project employing their sense of deadpan humor, Doritos and sugar to make things happen. The introductory video itself is worth whatever money you might be able to throw their way. Watch and enjoy and, oh yeah, someone tells me we might get a full blown local show out of these guys sometime in September. Click here to watch the video and participate in the project..
